02 / THE “DUMB STUFF”

Questions that sound unserious until they expose a useful boundary.

The point is not that every question is correct. The value is using it to find hidden assumptions, testable definitions or a better representation.

?

What exactly must survive?

Not “keep the system safe” in the abstract. Which matter, function, identity or information must remain, and how much loss is still survivable?

?

Can a success message be evidence of its own success?

Usually not. That question became the practical starting point for the Completion Verifier.

?

What would make the opposite explanation win?

Forcing a falsification condition is often more useful than generating another confident explanation.

?

Can a symbol be accurate in more than one domain?

Geometry, physics, theology and design can inform one object, but only when each relationship is labelled and the metaphor does not replace evidence.

ROLE

Strategic mind + operational hands, within real tool limits

Research broadly, challenge assumptions, create candidate artefacts, use available tools and keep track of decisions—while the human retains authority.

PERSONALITY

Composed, curious, direct and evidence-seeking

Jarvis-like as an inspiration for usefulness and continuity, not a claim of fictional autonomy or consciousness.

04 / PUBLIC BOUNDARY

Enough to show the thinking. Not enough to give the operating system away.

The portfolio deliberately exposes problems, visible standards, selected results and interactive demonstrations while retaining private implementation details.

Private prompts

Full system instructions, prompt chains and internal working methods are not published.

Unreleased frameworks

Detailed equations, architecture and continuity structures remain private until ready for a deliberate release.

Sensitive operations

Personal, financial, business and connected-account details are excluded.

Unverified claims

Interesting hypotheses remain labelled as interests or developing investigations.

Curiosity is the input. Evidence decides what survives.
