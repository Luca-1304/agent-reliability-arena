    DECISION 01

    Keep acceptance contracts independent from agent reports.

    A success-shaped receipt cannot define its own proof requirements.

    DECISION 02

    Use the latest relevant event.

    Recovery can replace an earlier failure, while a later rollback or failure can invalidate earlier success.

    DECISION 03

    Separate raw evidence from derived judgement.

    Reviewers can inspect what happened before accepting the evaluator’s conclusion.

    DECISION 04

    Prevent the specialist system from grading itself.

    Strategist and Auditor cannot mutate state; Operator cannot approve completion; Synthesiser cannot override verification.

    DECISION 05

    Validate experiment plumbing before claiming live-model results.

    Deterministic fixtures make logic, replay and fairness testable before provider variance is introduced.

    DECISION 06

    Expose the cost side of reliability.

    The specialist result is shown with its extra logical role calls instead of presenting improvement as free.

    08 / A REALISTIC FIRST 90 DAYS

    How the capability could become useful inside a team.

    This is a working approach, not a promise made without seeing the organisation’s systems, constraints or risk profile.

    DAYS 1–30

    Map the real failure surface.

    Understand the workflow before recommending AI.

    • Interview process owners and reviewers
    • Collect representative tasks and failures
    • Define acceptance contracts and risk classes
    • Establish baseline time, quality and review load
    DAYS 31–60

    Build a narrow shadow pilot.

    Test one valuable workflow without granting unsafe authority.

    • Create evaluation cases and trace capture
    • Prototype the smallest useful AI role
    • Keep consequential decisions human-approved
    • Measure edits, errors, recovery and reviewer effort
    DAYS 61–90

    Turn results into a decision.

    Show where to scale, redesign or stop.

    • Publish reproducible findings and limitations
    • Separate provider failures from workflow failures
    • Prioritise next use cases by evidence and reversibility
    • Leave a roadmap, runbook and ownership model
